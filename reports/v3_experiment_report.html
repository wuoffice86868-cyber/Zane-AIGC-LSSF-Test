1 What Changed
V1 — Rule-Based

Constraints & Format Rules

Rigid formula: every prompt opens with "Single continuous shot", closes with a stability anchor ("Sky remains still", "Bed frame stays anchored"). Fixed camera verbs. Scene-specific rules hardcoded.

30-45 words stability anchors 90% push forward DO NOT rules
"Single continuous shot. Wide shot of the infinity pool at golden hour. The camera pushes slowly forward along the pool edge. Water ripples softly in warm light. Sky and clouds remain completely still."
V3 — Description-Rich

Visual Detail & Atmosphere

Dropped formulaic opener & stability anchors (Seedance ignores these). Freed ~8 words for textures, light quality, materials, atmosphere. Scene description first, camera movement last.

25-40 words rich descriptions diverse cameras degree adverbs
"Luxury infinity pool merges with golden ocean horizon, palm fronds casting warm shadows on turquoise water, light ripples dancing across the surface. Camera slowly pushes forward along the pool edge."
Core shift: "tell the model what NOT to do" → "tell the model what the scene LOOKS like"

4 What Led to This Change
Round 1: V1 Baseline (19 samples)
Avg reward 70.8, pass rate 32%. V1 and V2_diverse scored almost identically (~71 each). Formula-based prompts hit a ceiling — changing camera moves within the same format didn't help.
Round 2: OPRO Optimization (16 samples)
Avg reward dropped to 62.9 (−8 points). OPRO added more constraint text ("maintain structural integrity") — Seedance ignores these. More constraint tokens = fewer description tokens = worse output.
Research Pivot
Studied Seedance official docs + community practices. Key findings: (1) Recommended structure is subject → scene → camera, not rules → format. (2) Negative prompts have zero effect. (3) Degree adverbs (slowly, gently) have strong effect.
V3 Hypothesis → Validated
Remove all constraints, fill with rich visual descriptions, put scene before camera. Result: +10.2 reward, pass rate 33% → 67%. Consistent across 2 independent runs.
5 Key Takeaways

1. Description > Constraint. Rich visual descriptions outperform format rules. Seedance responds to what you describe, not what you prohibit.
2. Consistent signal. Two runs, same delta (~+10). This isn't noise.
3. Motion is the biggest win. Motion score +1.5 drove the pass rate jump — fewer morphing/looping auto-fails.
4. Some scenes resist improvement. Lobby with humans → face_morphing regardless of prompt. Model-level limitation.
5. Sample size caveat. 24 samples show clear trend. Need 50+ for per-scene statistical confidence.
